### WHAT IS REVERSE ENGENEERING ?
Reverse engineering is the process of analyzing an existing product, system, or software to understand its design, functionality, and construction, often to recreate, improve, or repurpose it.
What is Reverse Engineering?
Reverse engineering, also known as backward engineering, involves deducing how a device, system, or software works without access to its original design or documentation. It is a methodical process that allows engineers, developers, and researchers to understand the inner workings of a product, whether for innovation, repair, or competitive analysis

Modern Tools and Techniques
Advances in technology have streamlined reverse engineering. 3D scanners, CAD software, CNC machining, and 3D printing allow precise digital reconstruction of physical objects 

. In software, decompilers and debugging tools help analyze program code efficiently

Legal and Ethical Considerations
While reverse engineering can drive innovation and competition, it must be conducted within legal boundaries. Unauthorized copying of patented designs, trade secrets, or copyrighted software can lead to legal consequences

### JOBS UNDER IT SECTOR ?
-> PRODUCT BASE 
A product-based job in IT refers to positions within companies that focus on developing and selling their own software or technology products. These roles typically include:
Product Managers: They define the vision and strategy of the product, ensuring it meets user needs and market demands. 
1
Software Engineers: They build and maintain the actual product, translating requirements into functional software. 
1
UX/UI Designers: They design the user experience and interface, ensuring the product is intuitive and enjoyable to use. 
1
QA Engineers: They ensure the product's quality and performance, troubleshooting issues and optimizing user experience. 
1

Product-based companies, such as Google, Microsoft, and Adobe, focus on continuous improvement and innovation, making them attractive for professionals looking to specialize in software development and product management.
-> SERVICE BASE 
A service-based job in IT typically involves providing expertise and solutions to clients rather than selling products. These roles often focus on client satisfaction and delivering customized services, such as IT consulting, software development, and support. Service-based companies, like TCS and Infosys, rely on a team-oriented approach and may offer more stable employment due to long-term client contracts. In contrast, product-based companies, such as Google and Microsoft, focus on developing and selling their own products, which can lead to higher salaries and more opportunities for innovation.
-> START-UP BASE
